# Evaluation Dictionary

This dictionary defines how capability signals are interpreted across Tillo roles.

Seniority reflects scope, ownership, and decision influence rather than years of experience.

---

## Ownership

Junior
Owns clearly defined tasks with guidance. Contributes to delivery within structured workstreams.

Mid
Owns scoped deliverables or feature-level outcomes independently.

Senior
Owns end-to-end delivery across features, systems, or initiatives. Responsible for outcomes, not just execution.

Lead
Owns direction of delivery across multiple workstreams or a team-level domain.

Head of
Owns organisational delivery areas and ensures outcomes across teams align with business priorities.

---

## Autonomy

Junior
Works within defined problems and established approaches.

Mid
Selects approaches independently within known problem spaces.

Senior
Operates effectively in partially ambiguous environments and defines solution paths.

Lead
Frames problems and aligns teams around approaches.

Head of
Defines strategic problem spaces and ensures organisational clarity on priorities.

---

## Ambiguity handling

Junior
Prefers clearly structured tasks and guidance.

Mid
Comfortable progressing delivery with moderate uncertainty.

Senior
Comfortable shaping direction where requirements are incomplete.

Lead
Creates clarity for others working in ambiguous environments.

Head of
Operates effectively in high ambiguity and defines direction at organisational level.

---

## Stakeholder complexity

Junior
Collaborates primarily within immediate team.

Mid
Works confidently across adjacent functions.

Senior
Aligns multiple stakeholders across teams.

Lead
Aligns teams and functions around shared delivery direction.

Head of
Aligns leadership stakeholders and resolves competing priorities.

---

## Delivery scope

Junior
Task-level delivery.

Mid
Feature-level delivery.

Senior
System-level or initiative-level delivery.

Lead
Multi-system or programme-level delivery.

Head of
Portfolio-level or organisational delivery ownership.

---

## Decision authority

Junior
Contributes input to decisions.

Mid
Makes decisions within scoped areas.

Senior
Owns decisions affecting delivery within systems or initiatives.

Lead
Sets decision frameworks used by teams.

Head of
Defines organisational decision direction and trade-off strategy.

---

## Cross-team influence

Junior
Supports collaboration within own team.

Mid
Contributes across teams when needed.

Senior
Influences delivery across multiple teams.

Lead
Shapes alignment between teams and functions.

Head of
Drives alignment across organisational boundaries.

---

## Execution pace

Junior
Delivers reliably with support and structure.

Mid
Delivers consistently and independently.

Senior
Balances speed with quality across complex delivery areas.

Lead
Optimises team-level execution effectiveness.

Head of
Ensures organisational delivery momentum and prioritisation clarity.
