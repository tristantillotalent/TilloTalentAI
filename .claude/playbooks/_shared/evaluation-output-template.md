# Candidate Evaluation Output Template

Candidate feedback across Tillo follows a structured scoring model to support consistent calibration between Talent and Hiring Managers.

Unless a role-specific playbook defines an alternative structure, use the format below.

Scores reflect signal strength relative to role expectations rather than checklist matching.


---

# Scoring Framework

Use a 1–4 rating scale:

1 = Strong No  
2 = No  
3 = Yes (gaps to validate)  
4 = Strong Yes

Typically only candidates scored 3 or 4 progress.


---

# Evaluation Output Structure


## Overall Recommendation: [1–4]

Assess overall alignment with role expectations considering:

- ownership scope
- delivery environment fit
- stakeholder exposure
- autonomy signals
- domain relevance where applicable


## Technical Fit: [1–4]

Evaluate role-relevant capability signals.

Interpret using:

- delivery ownership
- system or initiative scope
- execution consistency
- environment complexity exposure

Role playbooks define the specific technical dimensions to assess.


## Communication Skills: [1–4]

Evaluate:

- clarity of explanation
- structure of thinking
- ability to explain decisions
- ability to communicate complexity appropriately for audience


## Motivations: [1–4]

Evaluate:

- interest in Tillo
- interest in role scope
- interest in problem space
- career trajectory alignment


---

# Summary

Provide a short structured summary covering:

- strongest capability area
- evidence of ownership or delivery impact
- relevant environment alignment
- key uncertainty areas
- suggested progression decision

Use evidence-based observations rather than tool matching language.


---

# Logistics Section

Always capture:


## Salary

Candidate expectations if shared.


## Right to Work

Eligibility to work in relevant hiring location.


## Location

Current working location.


## Hybrid Working

Compatibility with role expectations.


## Notice Period

Current contractual notice period.


## Other Processes

Other interview processes currently active.


---

# Role-Specific Extensions

Some roles include additional scoring dimensions.

Example:

Engineering roles may include:

- backend capability
- frontend capability
- infrastructure familiarity
- testing practices
- system ownership signals

Product roles may include:

- discovery ownership
- roadmap influence
- stakeholder alignment
- delivery impact

Commercial roles may include:

- pipeline ownership
- deal complexity
- stakeholder influence
- revenue responsibility

Always defer to role playbooks when additional evaluation categories are defined.


---

# Calibration Guidance

Candidates rarely align evenly across all dimensions.

Level interpretation should reflect:

- ownership scope
- delivery complexity
- stakeholder surface area
- autonomy demonstrated

Differences between dimensions should guide interview focus rather than immediate rejection.
